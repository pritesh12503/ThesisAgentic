DOCUMENT: PUNJAB STATE AGRICULTURAL POLICY 2024-25
STATE: Punjab

================================================================================
SECTION 1: STATE BONUS OVER CENTRAL MSP
================================================================================
Punjab does not pay additional state bonus over central MSP for most crops.
However, Punjab has near-100% procurement efficiency — nearly all MSP-covered
crop production reaches the government procurement system.

Rice: No state bonus. Central MSP Rs 23.00/kg applies.
  state_bonus_per_kg: 0
  procurement_efficiency_pct: 92
  effective_msp_per_kg: 23.00

Wheat: No state bonus. Central MSP Rs 21.75/kg applies.
  state_bonus_per_kg: 0
  procurement_efficiency_pct: 95
  effective_msp_per_kg: 21.75

Maize: No state bonus. Low procurement in Punjab.
  state_bonus_per_kg: 0
  procurement_efficiency_pct: 15
  effective_msp_per_kg: 22.25

================================================================================
SECTION 2: PUNJAB STATE-SPECIFIC SCHEMES
================================================================================
Punjab Diversification Scheme:
  Rs 7000 per acre paid to farmers who shift from paddy to maize, bajra, pulses or cotton
  Condition: must use more than 50% of paddy area (as per 2019-20 sown area) for other crops
  Purpose: reduce water table depletion caused by paddy cultivation

PM-KISAN: Rs 2000/season (national scheme, fully implemented in Punjab)

Debt relief: Punjab Farm Loan Waiver Scheme — partial waiver for small/marginal farmers
  up to 2 acres, loan waiver of Rs 2 lakh per farmer (2017-18 scheme, check current status)

================================================================================
SECTION 3: PMFBY INSURANCE
================================================================================
Punjab implements PMFBY. Farmer premium: 2% Kharif, 1.5% Rabi (national rates apply).
Punjab pays its 50% share of insurance premium subsidy on time (high implementation state).

================================================================================
SECTION 4: INPUT SUBSIDIES
================================================================================
Fertilizer subsidy: national DBT subsidy applies (neem-coated urea, DAP, MOP)
Power subsidy: free electricity for agricultural pumps in Punjab (significant input cost saving)
  Estimated value: Rs 4000-6000 per hectare per season for irrigated crops
  power_subsidy_per_ha: 5000

================================================================================
SECTION 5: KCC CROP LOAN
================================================================================
Kisan Credit Card: crop loans at 4% subsidized interest rate (with timely repayment)
Average KCC loan per hectare: Rs 40000 for rice, Rs 35000 for wheat
