SECTION 2: PMFBY CROP INSURANCE — FARMER PREMIUM RATES
================================================================================
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY). Farmer pays capped premium.
Government (Centre + State) subsidises the balance of actuarial premium.

Kharif food crops and oilseeds: farmer premium = 2% of sum insured
Rabi food crops and oilseeds:   farmer premium = 1.5% of sum insured
Commercial and horticultural crops: farmer premium = 5% of sum insured

Sum insured is typically equal to Scale of Finance (crop loan amount per hectare).
Average sum insured per hectare: Rs 25,000 to Rs 45,000 depending on crop and state.

Kharif crops (2% premium): Rice, Maize, Jowar, Bajra, Ragi, Arhar/Tur, Moong,
  Urad, Groundnut, Sunflower, Soyabean, Sesamum, Cotton

Rabi crops (1.5% premium): Wheat, Barley, Gram, Masoor, Masoor, Linseed,
  Safflower, Rapeseed and Mustard

Commercial crops (5% premium): Cotton in some states, Sugarcane, Tobacco,
  Banana, Onion, Potato, Garlic, Ginger, Turmeric

Horticulture/vegetables (5% premium): Brinjal, Cabbage, Cauliflower, Pumpkin,
  Tomato, Bitter Gourd, Bottle Gourd, Sweet Potato, Tapioca,
  Coconut, Arecanut, Coffee, Black Pepper, Mango, Grapes, Orange,
  Pineapple, Guava, Lemon, Apple, Papaya

PMFBY sum insured per hectare (approximate, used for insurance cost calculation):
  Rice: 30000, Wheat: 28000, Maize: 22000, Jowar: 16000, Bajra: 14000
  Ragi: 15000, Barley: 18000, Arhar/Tur: 20000, Moong: 16000, Urad: 16000
  Gram: 20000, Masoor: 18000, Groundnut: 30000, Mustard: 20000
  Sunflower: 22000, Soyabean: 20000, Sesamum: 18000, Linseed: 17000
  Safflower: 18000, Cotton: 35000, Sugarcane: 40000

================================================================================
SECTION 3: PM-KISAN DIRECT INCOME SUPPORT
================================================================================
PM-KISAN: Rs 6000 per year paid directly to farmer families in three instalments.
Per season equivalent: Rs 2000 (approximately Rs 500 per hectare for average 4-ha farm).
Applies to: all landholding farmer families across all crops.
Not crop-specific. Added as flat per-season benefit.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: PROCUREMENT EFFICIENCY — NATIONAL AVERAGE
================================================================================
Not all states procure at MSP. National average procurement rates:
  Rice: ~30% of production procured at MSP nationally
  Wheat: ~35% of production procured at MSP nationally
  Pulses: ~10-15% procured under PM-AASHA
  Oilseeds: ~10% procured under PM-AASHA
  Cotton: ~20% procured via CCI

Implication: effective_price = MSP × procurement_probability + market_price × (1 - procurement_probability)
States with high procurement probability are listed in state-specific documents.
